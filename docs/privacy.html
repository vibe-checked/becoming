Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-05-31 · Last updated

Short version: Becoming does not collect, transmit, sell, share, or store any personal information. Your data lives on your device. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising SDKs, and no third-party trackers.

What this policy covers

This Privacy Policy describes how the Becoming mobile application ("Becoming", "we", "our", or "the app") handles information when you install and use it. It applies to the app published by Marcus Hsu and to the marketing site at kiddkevin00.github.io/becoming.

Information we collect

None that leaves your device. Becoming does not collect, request, or transmit any personally identifiable information. Specifically, the app does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or any account credentials.
  • Your location, contacts, photos, microphone audio, or camera images.
  • Your IP address, device identifier (IDFA / Advertising ID), or any other unique identifier.
  • Crash logs, analytics events, usage telemetry, or behavioral data.

Information stored on your device

The app stores your preferences and session history (selected theme, session duration, reflection responses, and optional notes) locally using your device's standard application storage. This data:

  • Never leaves your device.
  • Is removed when you delete the app or clear the app's storage in your device settings.
  • Is not backed up to any cloud service by us. Your device operating system (e.g. iCloud Backup on iOS, if you have it enabled) may include the app's storage in its own device backups — that is governed by your OS, not by us.

Audio and speech

Becoming uses your device's built-in text-to-speech engine (iOS AVSpeechSynthesizer) to read affirmations aloud. Speech synthesis happens entirely on-device. No audio is recorded, stored, or transmitted. The app does not request microphone access.

Third-party services

Becoming does not integrate any third-party advertising networks, analytics providers, crash reporters, attribution SDKs, social-login SDKs, or marketing tools.

The marketing site (kiddkevin00.github.io/becoming) is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect basic request logs (IP address, user agent, timestamp) as part of normal web hosting. See GitHub's privacy statement for details. We do not have access to those logs.

Children's privacy

Becoming is suitable for all ages and complies with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). Because the app collects no personal information from anyone — adults or children — there is nothing for us to delete, modify, or restrict on behalf of a minor.

Your rights

Under regulations such as the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), you have rights to access, correct, delete, or export personal data a company holds about you. Because Becoming holds no personal data about you, there is nothing to provide. Session data on your device is fully under your control: you can erase it by uninstalling the app.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. Material changes (for example, the introduction of any analytics or third-party SDKs) will be highlighted prominently and will require a new App Store submission, so users will see them as part of the standard app update process.
